## Strengthen electronic fundamentals
My internship also includes a project to build an analog light tracker robot from scratch, without using a microcontroller.
The objective of this project is to steer a small 3-wheeled car using light.
This robot uses two photoresistors to detect the light intensity on each side. 
The light intensity caught by each photoresistor is converted into a voltage signal, which is then processed by an operational amplifier configured as a comparator. 
This voltage signal is compared to a reference voltage set by a potentiometer, allowing the robot to determine which direction to turn based on the light intensity of each side.

### Components used
- 2 Photoresistors (LDR)
- 1 Potentiometer
- 1 LM324 Operational Amplifier
- 2 DC motors
- 1 3-wheeled chassis
- 1 Battery pack (4xAA)
- Various resistors and capacitors
- Breadboard and jumper wires
- 1 Switch
- 2 Diodes (1N4007)
- 2 Transistors (2N2222)
- 2 Light Emitting Diodes (LEDs)

### Version with PCB
After successfully building and testing the breadboard prototype, I made a custom PCB to enhance the robot's reliability and compactness using KiCad, FlatCAM and Candle softwares with a CNC milling machine.
I learned how to design a PCB layout, generate G-code, and use the CNC machine to mill the PCB.
